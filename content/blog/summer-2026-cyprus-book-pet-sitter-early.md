Every July, the same thing happens. Pet owners across Cyprus start searching for someone to care for their dog or cat while they travel. By mid-July, most experienced sitters are fully booked. By August, the messages in Facebook groups become increasingly desperate: "Does ANYONE know a pet sitter available this weekend in Limassol?"

This is not a surprise. It happens every single summer. And every single summer, people wait too long.

## Why Summer Is Different

Cyprus receives over four million tourists per year, with the heaviest concentration between June and September. But the demand spike for pet sitters is not driven by tourists — it is driven by residents and expats leaving the island for summer holidays.

The expat population in Limassol and Paphos is particularly affected. Many British, Russian, and Northern European expats travel home during the summer months, and they need someone to care for their pets while they are away. At the same time, Cypriot families travel for the August holiday period.

The result is that demand for pet sitting in July and August is three to four times higher than the rest of the year, while the supply of available sitters remains roughly constant. The best sitters — the ones with strong reviews, verified profiles, and experience — are booked out weeks in advance.

## When to Book

For travel in July or August 2026, book your sitter by mid-May at the latest. For travel in June or September, booking three to four weeks ahead is usually sufficient.

For Christmas and New Year travel, book by late November.

The penalty for booking late is not just unavailability. Last-minute bookings often mean accepting a sitter you have not met, skipping the meet-and-greet, or paying a premium rate. None of these are ideal when you are leaving your pet in someone else's care.

## What to Look For in a Summer Sitter

Summer in Cyprus brings specific challenges that a good sitter should understand.

Heat management is critical. Temperatures in July and August regularly exceed 35°C and can reach 40°C or higher. Dogs should not be walked during midday heat. A responsible sitter will walk your dog early in the morning (before 8 AM) and in the evening (after 7 PM), and ensure constant access to fresh water and shade.

Snake awareness matters in rural areas. Cyprus has the blunt-nosed viper, which is active in summer months. Sitters who walk dogs in rural or semi-rural areas — common around Paphos, the Troodos foothills, and Parekklisia — should know the basic precautions and the location of the nearest emergency vet.

For cats, the main summer risk is dehydration. A cat left alone with insufficient water in a hot apartment can become dehydrated within hours. If your sitter is doing drop-in visits, twice daily is the minimum during peak summer heat — not once.

Ask any prospective sitter how they handle heat, and listen to whether their answer demonstrates actual awareness or a generic reassurance.

## The Cost of Waiting

Beyond availability, there is a practical cost to last-minute bookings. Sitters who are booked well in advance often offer their standard rates. Sitters who have a few remaining slots during peak season may charge a premium — sometimes 20 to 30 percent above their normal rate. This is normal supply-and-demand pricing, but it is avoidable if you book early.
