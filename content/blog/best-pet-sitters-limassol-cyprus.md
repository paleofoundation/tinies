## What to Look For

Regardless of which platform you use, evaluate any pet sitter on these criteria before booking.

Verification matters. Has the sitter submitted ID? Have they passed any kind of background or reference check? Platforms that require identity verification before approval — like Tinies and PetBacker — filter out a significant number of unreliable sitters before you ever see their profile.

Reviews from real bookings are more reliable than testimonials. Look for platforms where reviews are tied to completed, verified bookings rather than self-reported endorsements.

A meet-and-greet before the first booking is non-negotiable for overnight or extended care. Any sitter who resists meeting your pet in advance is not someone you want caring for your animal unsupervised. Tinies includes a [meet-and-greet process](/how-it-works) as part of its booking flow.

Insurance and payment protection matter more than you think they will — until something goes wrong. Platforms that hold payment in escrow and release it only after the booking is completed give you recourse that a cash-in-hand arrangement does not.

Pricing varies significantly depending on the service type and platform.

Drop-in visits — where a sitter comes to your home once or twice daily — typically run €10 to €20 per visit through marketplace platforms. Overnight in-home sitting, where the sitter stays at your home, ranges from €25 to €50 per night depending on the number of pets and specific requirements. Dog walking is usually €8 to €15 per walk.

## Questions to Ask Before You Book

Before confirming any pet sitter, ask these questions — in person, by message, or during the meet-and-greet.

How many other animals will be in your care at the same time? A sitter managing six dogs simultaneously cannot give your pet the same attention as one managing two. What is your plan if my pet gets sick or injured while I am away? The answer should include a specific veterinary clinic, not a vague assurance. Can you accommodate my pet's medication, dietary, or behavioral needs? If your cat needs daily medication or your dog has separation anxiety, the sitter needs to know this upfront and confirm they can handle it. Will you send daily updates with photos? This is standard on most platforms and should be expected, not negotiated.
