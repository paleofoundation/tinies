If you love animals and live in Cyprus, you can earn a meaningful income caring for other people's pets. Dog walking, pet sitting, overnight boarding, daycare, and drop-in visits are all services that pet owners in Cyprus need — and are willing to pay for.

This guide covers what you can realistically earn, how to get started, and how to choose the right platform.

## What can you actually earn?

Let us do the maths with real Cyprus market prices.

**Scenario 1: Part-time dog walker (weekday mornings)**

You walk three dogs per morning, five days per week. Each walk is thirty minutes at EUR 15.

- 3 walks × EUR 15 × 5 days = EUR 225/week
- Monthly: approximately EUR 900
- After 12% Tinies commission: EUR 792/month

**Scenario 2: Part-time pet sitter (evenings and weekends)**

You take two boarding dogs per week (Thursday to Sunday, three nights each) at EUR 25/night.

- 2 dogs × EUR 25 × 3 nights = EUR 150/week
- Monthly: approximately EUR 600
- After 12% commission: EUR 528/month

**Scenario 3: Full-time provider (walks + boarding + daycare)**

You walk three dogs per morning (EUR 15 each), take one daycare dog three days per week (EUR 20/day), and board one dog every other weekend (EUR 25/night × 2 nights).

- Walks: EUR 225/week
- Daycare: EUR 60/week
- Boarding: EUR 25/week (averaged)
- Total: EUR 310/week
- Monthly: approximately EUR 1,240
- After 12% commission: EUR 1,091/month

These are conservative estimates using median Cyprus prices. Providers who build a strong review history and offer premium services (training, grooming add-ons, specialised care) can earn EUR 1,500-2,000+ per month.

## How commission affects your earnings

The percentage a platform takes from your bookings matters more than you think. Here is how the same EUR 25/night boarding booking plays out across platforms:

| Platform | Commission | You keep | Over 100 bookings |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tinies | 12% | EUR 22.00 | EUR 2,200 |
| [Rover](https://www.rover.com/) | ~20% | EUR 20.00 | EUR 2,000 |
| [PetBacker](https://www.petbacker.com/) (new) | 25% | EUR 18.75 | EUR 1,875 |
| PetBacker (established) | 15-20% | EUR 20-21.25 | EUR 2,000-2,125 |

On PetBacker, new providers start at a 25% commission rate. It drops as you complete more bookings — but only to 15% after 3,001 jobs. And if PetBacker discovers you are also working on another platform, the commission jumps to 40%.

On Tinies, the rate is 12% from day one. No tiered penalties. No exclusivity requirements. You are free to work on as many platforms as you like.

The commission difference between 12% and 25% on EUR 1,000/month of bookings is EUR 130/month — that is EUR 1,560/year more in your pocket.

- Upload at least five high-quality photos
- Respond to messages quickly — your response rate is visible on your profile
- Offer a free meet-and-greet to build trust before the first booking

## What makes a great provider profile

We have seen what works. The providers who get the most bookings have:

**A real, friendly photo.** Not a selfie in sunglasses. Not a photo of their dog. A clear, well-lit photo of themselves smiling.

**A detailed bio.** Not "I love animals." Instead: "I have been caring for dogs and cats for five years. I live in a ground-floor flat in Limassol with a private garden. I am home most days and have experience with senior dogs, puppies, and cats with medical needs."

**Multiple photos of their space.** Owners want to see where their pet will be. A garden with a fence. A clean living room with a dog bed. A cat-proofed balcony.

**Competitive but not cheap pricing.** Undercutting the market attracts bargain hunters, not loyal clients. Price at or slightly below the area average to start, then raise once you have five or more reviews.

**Fast responses.** Providers who respond within an hour get significantly more bookings than those who take a day. Turn on notifications.
