When you need to leave your dog for a few days or longer, the decision usually comes down to three options: a traditional boarding kennel, a home-based sitter, or a marketplace platform that connects you with verified local caregivers. Each has real advantages and real drawbacks, and the right choice depends on your dog's temperament, your budget, and how much control you want over the experience.

Here is what each option actually looks like in Cyprus in 2026.

## Traditional Boarding Kennels

Cyprus has several established boarding kennels. [RoseWood Kennels](https://rosewoodkennelscyprus.com/) near Nicosia has operated for over 25 years on a three-acre property with indoor/outdoor runs, heated in winter and shaded in summer. [Dogs Hotel Cyprus](https://dogshotelcy.com/) in Kambia offers a five-acre facility with a pool. [Asgata Redrock Kennels](https://www.asgataredrockkennels.com/home) in the Limassol district provides 24-hour supervision with outdoor play areas. In Paphos, [Pooches Palace](https://www.facebook.com/Poochespalacekennels/) in Kallepeia offers boutique boarding with free pickup and return.

Kennel pricing in Cyprus typically ranges from €15 to €30 per night depending on the facility, the size of the run, and any extras like grooming or additional walks. Most kennels require up-to-date vaccinations and a trial visit.

The advantage of kennels is professional infrastructure, veterinary proximity, and the assurance that your dog is in a purpose-built facility with experienced staff. The disadvantage is the environment. Kennels are noisy. Dogs are surrounded by other barking dogs, in an unfamiliar space, without the comfort of home. For dogs who are anxious, elderly, or have never been boarded, this can be highly stressful.

## Home-Based Dog Boarding

Home boarding means your dog stays in someone else's house — sleeping on their couch, joining their routine, and receiving one-on-one attention in a domestic environment. This is the model used by platforms like [PetBacker](https://www.petbacker.com/s/dog-boarding/--cyprus) and [Tinies](/services).

Pricing for home boarding through marketplace platforms in Cyprus ranges from €15 to €35 per night. The sitter sets their own rate, and the platform handles payment, verification, and insurance.

The advantage is a home environment. Your dog sleeps indoors, gets individual attention, and maintains something closer to a normal routine. For dogs who are anxious, reactive to other dogs, or simply more comfortable on a sofa than in a kennel run, home boarding is significantly less stressful.

The disadvantage is variability. The quality of care depends entirely on the individual sitter. This is where platform verification, reviews, and meet-and-greets become essential. Any reputable platform will allow — and encourage — a pre-booking visit where your dog meets the sitter in their home. If you are not comfortable with the space, the other animals present, or the sitter's understanding of your dog's needs, do not book.

## Marketplace Platforms Compared

Three marketplace platforms currently operate in Cyprus for dog boarding.

[PetBacker](https://www.petbacker.com/s/dog-boarding/--cyprus) is a global platform with 48 listed dog boarding providers across Cyprus. It includes payment protection, insurance, and GPS-tracked dog walking for applicable bookings. Sitters go through identity verification. Prices start around €15 per night.

[TrustedHousesitters](https://www.trustedhousesitters.com/house-and-pet-sitting-assignments/cyprus/) operates on a different model — sitters provide care for free in exchange for accommodation in your home. This works well for longer trips where you want someone staying in your house, but you pay an annual membership fee (starting around €129 for owners) and have less flexibility on short-notice bookings.

## Which Is Right for Your Dog?

If your dog is sociable, high-energy, and enjoys being around other dogs, a well-run kennel with group play can be a positive experience. Facilities like RoseWood and Dogs Hotel Cyprus offer the space and structure for active dogs.

If your dog is anxious, elderly, recently adopted, or does not do well in noisy environments, home boarding or in-home sitting (where the sitter comes to your house) is significantly better. The reduced stimulation and familiar-feeling environment makes a measurable difference in stress levels.

If you are going on a long trip and want someone living in your home, TrustedHousesitters is worth considering for the cost savings alone. ## Before You Book

Visit the facility or the sitter's home in person before the first booking. Ask how many other dogs will be present at the same time. Confirm that the sitter or facility can handle your dog's specific needs — medication, dietary restrictions, leash reactivity, separation anxiety. Leave emergency contact details and your vet's information. Book early during summer and holiday periods — the best sitters and kennels fill up weeks in advance.
