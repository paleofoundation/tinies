The cost of pet care in Cyprus depends on what you need, where you are, and who you hire. Prices have risen over the past two years alongside the general cost of living, but Cyprus remains significantly cheaper than the UK or Northern Europe for equivalent services.

Here is what you can realistically expect to pay in 2026 across the main service types.

## Dog Walking

A single dog walk of 30 to 60 minutes typically costs €8 to €15 in Limassol and Nicosia, and €7 to €12 in Larnaca and Paphos. Group walks (where your dog walks with one or two others) are at the lower end. Solo walks are at the higher end.

Regular packages — three to five walks per week — often come with a discount. Some walkers on [Tinies](/services) offer weekly packages at 10 to 20 percent below the per-walk rate.

For reference, dog walking in central London costs £12 to £20 per walk (roughly €14 to €23). Cyprus prices are roughly 30 to 40 percent lower for equivalent service.

## Drop-In Visits (Cat Sitting)

A drop-in visit — where a sitter comes to your home for 20 to 30 minutes to feed your cat, refresh water, clean the litter box, and check on the animal — costs €10 to €20 per visit across most of Cyprus.

For cat owners going away for a few days, one visit per day is usually sufficient for healthy adult cats. For longer trips or cats with medical needs, twice-daily visits are recommended. ## Overnight In-Home Sitting

This is where a sitter stays in your home overnight, providing continuous care for your pet. It is the premium option and the least stressful for both dogs and cats.

Overnight in-home sitting in Cyprus costs €25 to €50 per night. The price varies by location (Limassol tends to be slightly higher than Larnaca or Paphos), the number of pets, and any special requirements. Some sitters charge a small additional fee for multiple pets.

On [Tinies](/services), providers set their own rates. The platform adds no hidden fees — the 12 percent commission is included in the listed price. Approximately 90 percent of that commission goes to [animal rescue in Cyprus](/giving).

## Dog Boarding (Sitter's Home)

If you prefer your dog to stay at the sitter's home rather than having someone come to yours, pricing ranges from €15 to €35 per night on marketplace platforms like [PetBacker](https://www.petbacker.com/s/dog-boarding/--cyprus) and Tinies.

This option works well for sociable dogs who enjoy new environments and other animals. It is less ideal for anxious dogs, senior dogs, or dogs with behavioral issues. See our [dog boarding comparison guide](/blog/dog-boarding-cyprus-kennels-vs-sitters) for a full breakdown of kennels vs home boarding.

## Kennel Boarding

Traditional kennels in Cyprus charge €15 to €30 per night depending on the facility. Premium options with larger runs, air conditioning, and additional exercise time are at the upper end. Facilities like [RoseWood Kennels](https://rosewoodkennelscyprus.com/) near Nicosia and [Dogs Hotel Cyprus](https://dogshotelcy.com/) in Kambia are among the most established.

Most kennels require proof of current vaccinations and may charge extra for grooming, medication administration, or extended outdoor time.

## Price Differences by District

Limassol and Nicosia are the most expensive districts for pet care, reflecting higher demand and cost of living. Paphos, Larnaca, and Famagusta are generally 10 to 20 percent cheaper for equivalent services.

The difference is most pronounced for in-home sitting and dog walking. Kennel prices are more standardized across the island.

## Platform Fees and Hidden Costs

Different platforms handle fees differently.

On [PetBacker](https://www.petbacker.com/s/pet-sitters/limassol--cyprus), the sitter's listed price includes the platform's service fee. Payment is held in escrow and released upon completion.

On [TrustedHousesitters](https://www.trustedhousesitters.com/house-and-pet-sitters/cyprus/), there is no per-booking fee — but you pay an annual membership (starting around €129 for pet owners). Sitters care for your pet for free in exchange for accommodation.

On [Tinies](/services), the price you see is the price you pay. The 12 percent commission is built in, with no additional booking fees, service charges, or cancellation penalties beyond the stated policy. The key difference: [most of that commission funds animal rescue](/blog/tinies-model-booking-fee-funds-sanctuary).

With informal arrangements — Facebook groups, word of mouth, cash-in-hand — there are no fees, but there is also no insurance, no verification, no payment protection, and no recourse if something goes wrong.

## Is It Worth Paying More for a Platform?

The price difference between booking through a verified platform and finding someone on Facebook is usually €3 to €8 per booking. That margin buys you identity-verified sitters, payment protection, booking records, and — on Tinies — the knowledge that your booking directly supports sanctuary operations.

For a one-off dog walk, the stakes are low and an informal arrangement may be fine. For overnight care, multi-day boarding, or care for a pet with medical needs, the protection of a platform is worth the marginal cost.
