The demand for pet care in Cyprus is growing faster than the supply of providers. Over four million tourists visit the island each year. The expat population continues to grow, particularly in Limassol and Paphos. Pet ownership is rising. And most pet owners still rely on informal arrangements — neighbors, Facebook groups, word of mouth — because there are not enough professional sitters available.

This is an opportunity. If you like animals, have a flexible schedule, and want to earn consistent side income — or build a full-time pet care business — the market is wide open.

## What You Can Realistically Earn

Earnings depend on the services you offer, your location, and how many hours you commit.

Dog walking at €10 to €15 per walk, with three to four walks per day, five days per week, generates €600 to €1,200 per month. Many walkers start with one or two regular clients and build from there.

Drop-in cat sitting at €12 to €18 per visit, with two to three visits per day during peak periods, generates €500 to €800 per month — primarily concentrated around holiday seasons when demand spikes.

Overnight in-home sitting at €30 to €45 per night is the highest-earning service. A sitter who is booked for 15 nights per month earns €450 to €675. During July, August, and the December holiday period, occupancy rates climb significantly.

Home boarding — hosting dogs in your own home — at €20 to €35 per night can generate €600 to €1,050 per month at 50 percent occupancy. If you have space for two dogs simultaneously, the income doubles.

The most successful pet sitters in Cyprus combine multiple service types: regular dog walking for weekday income, overnight sitting for holiday periods, and drop-in visits for cat clients. A sitter offering all three and maintaining a steady client base can earn €1,000 to €2,000 per month working part-time hours.

## How to Get Started

You do not need formal qualifications to become a pet sitter in Cyprus. What you need is reliability, genuine comfort with animals, and a way for clients to find you.

Step one is deciding what services to offer. Start with what you are most comfortable with. If you have a fenced garden and a dog-friendly home, home boarding is natural. If you live in an apartment, drop-in visits and dog walking are more practical.

Step two is listing yourself on a platform. [Tinies](/for-providers) is built specifically for Cyprus. Signing up is free. You create a profile describing your services, set your own rates, upload photos of your space (for boarding) or yourself (for walking and sitting), and begin receiving booking requests. Tinies handles payment processing, provides booking protection, and markets your profile to pet owners in your area.

You can also list on [PetBacker](https://www.petbacker.com/s/pet-sitters/limassol--cyprus), which has a larger global audience but less Cyprus-specific focus.

Step three is getting your first reviews. The first three to five reviews are the hardest to earn and the most important for your profile visibility. Consider offering a modest introductory rate to your first few clients in exchange for honest reviews. Once you have a track record, organic demand grows.

## What Makes a Great Profile

The profiles that get the most bookings share common traits.

Clear, well-lit photos — of you with animals, of your home or garden if you offer boarding, of you on a walk. Avoid generic stock images. Clients want to see where their pet will be and who will be caring for it.

A specific, honest bio. "I love animals" is not a differentiator. "I have two rescue cats of my own, a fenced garden in Germasogeia, and I have been walking dogs in the neighborhood for three years" is.

Listed rates and availability. Clients who have to message you to learn your price will often move on to someone who has listed theirs. Be transparent.

Quick response times. Sitters who respond to booking requests within a few hours get significantly more confirmed bookings than those who take days.

## Peak Periods to Prepare For

Demand for pet sitting in Cyprus peaks during three periods: Easter (March or April), summer holidays (mid-June through August), and the Christmas-New Year period (mid-December through early January).

If you are available during these windows and your profile is active with reviews, you will be booked. The key is having your profile established and reviewed before peak season arrives — not scrambling to set one up the week before.
