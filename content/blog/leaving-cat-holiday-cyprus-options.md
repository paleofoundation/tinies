You have a flight booked. The hotel is sorted. And now the question that every cat owner in Cyprus eventually faces: what do you do with the cat?

Cats are not dogs. Most cats do not travel well, do not enjoy new environments, and are deeply attached to their territory. The best solution for almost every cat is one that keeps them in their own home with someone checking in regularly. But the specifics matter, and the options available in Cyprus are more varied than most people realize.

## Option 1: In-Home Cat Sitting

This is the gold standard. A sitter comes to your home — once or twice a day, or stays overnight — to feed your cat, clean the litter box, check water, and provide company. Your cat stays in their familiar environment, sleeps in their usual spot, and experiences minimal disruption.

In-home sitting typically costs €10 to €20 per visit for drop-ins, or €25 to €50 per night for overnight stays. On [Tinies](/services), you can search for sitters who specifically offer cat care in your area, read verified reviews, and book securely with payment protection.

The main advantage is that this is the least stressful option for your cat. The main risk is trusting someone with access to your home, which is why platforms with ID verification and secure payment — rather than informal Facebook arrangements — provide a meaningful layer of protection.

For cats who are shy, semi-feral, or recently adopted, in-home sitting is the only realistic option. A new environment would cause extreme stress. If your cat falls into this category, make sure the sitter meets your cat in advance and understands that the cat may hide and refuse interaction — that is normal, not a problem.

## Option 2: Staying With a Friend or Neighbor

The most common option in Cyprus, and often the most convenient. If you have a trusted neighbor who knows your cat, this can work well — especially for short trips of a few days.

The downside is that the quality of care is entirely dependent on the individual, there is no accountability structure, and cats who are moved to someone else's home may refuse to eat, hide for days, or attempt to escape. If your cat stays in your home and the neighbor visits, the outcome is better.

If you go this route, leave detailed written instructions (feeding amounts, medication schedules, vet contact information, your cat's hiding spots) rather than relying on verbal explanations that will be forgotten by day two.

## Option 3: A Cattery or Boarding Facility

Cyprus has a limited number of dedicated cat boarding facilities. Most boarding kennels — like [RoseWood Kennels](https://rosewoodkennelscyprus.com/) near Nicosia, [Dogs Hotel Cyprus](https://dogshotelcy.com/) in Kambia, or [Pet People](https://www.petpeoplecyprus.com/pethotel.php) — primarily serve dogs, though some accept cats.

Catteries typically cost €10 to €25 per day depending on the facility and the size of the enclosure. The advantage is professional supervision and veterinary access. The disadvantage is that most cats find the experience deeply stressful — new smells, new sounds, proximity to other animals, and the loss of their territory.

For sociable, confident cats who adapt easily to new environments, a good cattery can work. For the majority of cats — especially indoor cats, shy cats, or older cats — the stress of boarding outweighs the benefits. A study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://journals.sagepub.com/home/jfm) found that environmental changes are one of the primary triggers of stress-related illness in cats, including urinary issues and appetite loss.

## How Long Can You Leave a Cat Alone?

A healthy adult cat can manage alone for 24 to 48 hours with sufficient food, water, and a clean litter box. Beyond 48 hours, daily check-ins are essential — not just for feeding but for monitoring health. Cats can develop urinary blockages, injuries, or illness that worsen rapidly without detection.

For trips of one to three days, a single daily visit is usually sufficient. For trips of four days or more, twice-daily visits or overnight sitting is recommended. For trips longer than a week, overnight in-home sitting provides the most reliable care.

Kittens, elderly cats, cats with medical conditions, and recently adopted cats should never be left without daily in-person care, even for short trips.

## Before You Go: A Checklist

Leave the sitter or neighbor with your veterinarian's name, address, and phone number. Leave written feeding instructions including brand, quantity, and schedule. Show the sitter where your cat hides (under the bed, in the closet, on top of the wardrobe) so they do not panic when they cannot find the cat. Leave a spare key with a second person in case the primary sitter has an emergency. Make sure your cat's microchip information is current in case of escape.

## Book Before Peak Season

Summer and the December holiday period are the busiest times for pet sitters in Cyprus. If you are traveling during July, August, or over Christmas and New Year, book your sitter at least three to four weeks in advance. Last-minute searches during peak periods often result in no availability.
