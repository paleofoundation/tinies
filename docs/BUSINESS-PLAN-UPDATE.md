Tinies — Business Plan Update: Mission & Revenue Corrections
Replace Section 1 (Executive Summary) with:
Tinies is a pet services marketplace and international animal adoption platform that exists for one reason: to fund animal sanctuaries in Cyprus.
Gardens of St Gertrude, a cat sanctuary in Parekklisia caring for 92 cats, has been funded entirely out of pocket by its founder and the Paleo Foundation since its inception. Not a single external donation has ever been accepted. Tinies changes that — not by asking for handouts, but by building a platform that generates revenue through real services that real people need, and directing that revenue to the animals.
The platform does two things: it connects pet owners in Cyprus with verified, reviewed local service providers (walking, sitting, boarding, drop-ins, daycare), and it operates a professional international adoption coordination service that places rescue animals from Cyprus into homes across Europe. Every booking generates a commission. Approximately 90% of that commission funds Gardens of St Gertrude and other Cyprus animal sanctuaries (Malcolm's Cat Sanctuary, Patch of Heaven, and others as they join). The remaining 10% covers the platform's operating costs (hosting, payment processing, SMS, email).
This is not a marketplace that donates to charity. This is a charity that built a marketplace to fund itself. That distinction is the core of the brand and the reason no competitor can replicate what Tinies does.

3.1 Tinies Services (Local Pet Care Marketplace)
Pet owners search for verified, reviewed providers offering dog walking, pet sitting, overnight boarding, drop-in visits, and daycare. Providers list for free. Tinies takes a 12% commission on every completed booking.
Here is where the money goes on a EUR 50 booking:

The provider gets their full earning. The pet owner gets trusted, verified care. The cats get fed. The platform costs are covered. Everyone wins.
The commission is deliberately set at 12% — lower than Rover's 20-27% — because the competitive alternative in Cyprus is not another platform but zero-fee Facebook groups. The 12% must feel worth it because the platform delivers vetted customers, handles payment, provides review infrastructure, and offers dispute resolution. And because pet owners know their booking is funding animal rescue, not a tech company's profit margin.

Operating Costs Covered by the ~10%
The platform's operating costs are minimal by design:

Vercel hosting: EUR 0-20/month
Supabase database: EUR 0-25/month
Stripe fees: ~2.9% per transaction (unavoidable)
Twilio SMS: EUR 20-80/month
Email service: EUR 0-40/month
Domain: EUR 15/year

Total: approximately EUR 50-200/month depending on volume. Everything above operating costs goes to the sanctuaries.
What This Means in Practice
At 800 bookings/month (Year 1 target) with EUR 50 average:

To sanctuaries: ~EUR 4,320/month (~EUR 51,840/year)
To operating costs: ~EUR 480/month

Plus Guardian subscriptions, round-ups, and signup donations flowing directly to sanctuaries on top of that.
This is not theoretical charity. This is a feeding schedule, vet bills, medication, litter, and shelter maintenance for 92 cats and counting — funded by a marketplace that people actually want to use.
