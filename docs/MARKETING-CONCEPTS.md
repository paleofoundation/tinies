Tinies — Marketing Concepts (Updated)
The Real Story (Use Everywhere)
Tinies is not a tech company that donates to charity. Tinies is a cat sanctuary that built a tech company to feed its cats.
Gardens of St Gertrude is a cat sanctuary in Parekklisia, Cyprus. 92 cats. Funded 100% out of pocket by its founder. Zero external donations ever accepted — until now. Instead of asking for money, she built a platform. Every time someone books a dog walker, a pet sitter, or a boarding stay through Tinies, approximately 90% of the platform's commission goes directly to feeding, sheltering, and providing vet care for rescue animals.
That's the story. Everything else is marketing.

"Karen Pendergrass runs a cat sanctuary in Cyprus with 92 cats. She and her family have funded it entirely out of pocket for years. Instead of starting a GoFundMe, she built a pet services marketplace. Now, every time someone in Cyprus books a dog walker or adopts a rescue animal through her platform, 90% of the commission feeds her cats. The Transparency Story
Why It Matters More Than Anything Else
People are tired of "we donate a portion of proceeds to charity." They've heard it a thousand times and they don't believe it. What they've never seen is:
"Your EUR 50 dog walking booking generated EUR 5.40 for animal rescue. That bought 2kg of cat food — enough to feed 10 cats for a day. Here's a photo of them eating it."
That specificity is what Charity Water does with wells. It's what Tinies does with cats.
The /giving Page as Marketing Tool
The giving page is not buried in a submenu. It's in the main navigation. It's linked from every booking confirmation. It updates in real time. It shows:

Exact total donated (EUR 2,847.32, not "almost EUR 3,000")
Per-sanctuary breakdown with percentages
What the money bought this month (kg of food, vet visits, surgeries, medications)
Photos from the sanctuaries — real cats, real bowls, real food
A live feed of recent activity ("A dog walking booking just generated EUR 4.80 for animal rescue")

This page is shareable. When someone asks "where does the money go?" the answer is a URL. When a journalist asks for proof, the answer is a URL. When a potential Guardian subscriber wants to see impact before committing EUR 5/month, the answer is a URL.
Post-Booking Impact Message
Every booking confirmation includes:
"Your booking generated EUR {amount} for animal rescue at {sanctuary_name}. That's roughly {equivalent} — enough to {specific_impact}."
Examples:

"EUR 5.40 → ~2kg of cat food → feeds 10 cats for a day"
"EUR 7.20 → covers one vet checkup for a rescue kitten"
"EUR 3.60 → one day of litter for the sanctuary"

This turns a transaction into a story. People screenshot these and share them.

Target Audiences & Messaging
1. Cyprus Pet Owners (local services)
Message: "Find trusted care for your tiny. And know that your booking feeds rescue animals."
Channel: Cyprus expat Facebook groups, Google search (SEO), Instagram
Hook: The trust/verification angle first, the giving angle second. They need a dog walker. The fact that it also feeds cats is the bonus that makes them choose Tinies over asking on Facebook.
2. International Adopters (UK, Germany, Netherlands)
Message: "Adopt a tiny from Cyprus. We handle everything — vet prep, EU passport, transport, delivery to your door."
Channel: UK/EU rescue Facebook groups, Reddit (r/cats, r/dogs, r/rescueanimals), Google search, Instagram
Hook: Beautiful animal photos + the convenience of end-to-end logistics. "You don't have to figure out how to get a cat from Cyprus to London. We do it."
3. Animal Lovers Who Want to Help (Tinies Guardians)
Message: "Feed 92 cats for EUR 5/month. See exactly where your money goes."
Channel: Giving page, post-booking prompts, email, Instagram stories
Hook: Charity Water-level transparency. "This isn't a faceless charity. This is a woman with 92 cats who built a tech platform to feed them. Here are their names. Here's what they ate today."
4. Rescue Organizations in Cyprus
Message: "List your animals for free. Reach adopters across Europe. We handle international logistics."
Channel: Direct outreach (Karen's existing network), rescue community Facebook groups
Hook: Free international exposure + professional logistics coordination they can't do alone.
5. Pet Service Providers in Cyprus
Message: "List for free. We bring you customers. You only pay 12% when you earn. And that 12% feeds rescue animals."
Channel: Bazaraki outreach, Instagram DMs, Google Maps outreach
Hook: Free listings + the feel-good factor of knowing their commission feeds cats.

PR Angles (For Cyprus Mail, SigmaLive, BBC, Guardian)

"The woman who built a tech company to feed 92 cats" — Founder profile. Human interest. International appeal.
"Cyprus's stray cat crisis gets a tech solution" — Problem/solution angle. Connects to the broader animal welfare story.
"The pet marketplace where 90% of profits feed rescue animals" — Business model story. Contrast with Rover ($2.3B to Blackstone) vs. Tinies (to cats).
"From Cyprus to London: How rescue cats are finding homes across Europe" — International adoption story. First completed placement becomes the story.
"Book a dog walker, feed a sanctuary: The Tinies model" — Explainer piece. How the money flows from booking to bowl.
